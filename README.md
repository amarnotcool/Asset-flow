# AssetFlow

**Enterprise Asset & Resource Management System**


## 📖 Overview

AssetFlow is a centralized ERP platform designed to simplify and digitize how organizations track, allocate, and maintain their physical assets and shared resources. Built for any industry—from schools and hospitals to corporate offices and factories—AssetFlow eliminates the manual inefficiencies of spreadsheets and paper logs.

The platform delivers structured asset lifecycles, centralized resource booking, and real-time visibility into asset conditions and assignments, all driven by a secure, role-based architecture.

## ✨ Key Features

* **Full Asset Lifecycle Tracking:** Monitor assets as they transition through states: *Available, Allocated, Reserved, Under Maintenance, Lost, Retired,* and *Disposed*.
* **Conflict-Free Allocation:** Assign assets to employees or departments with built-in validation to strictly prevent double-allocations.
* **Resource Booking Engine:** Book shared resources (rooms, vehicles, equipment) via time slots with automatic overlap prevention.
* **Maintenance Workflows:** Route repair requests through a dedicated approval process before any work begins, automatically updating asset availability.
* **Structured Audit Cycles:** Run scheduled inventory audits with assigned auditors, verification checklists, and auto-generated discrepancy reports.
* **Actionable Analytics & KPIs:** Access real-time dashboards tracking overdue returns, peak booking windows, and maintenance frequencies.

## 👥 User Roles & Permissions

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| **Admin** | Manages organizational master data (departments, categories, employee directory), triggers audit cycles, and promotes standard employee accounts to managerial roles. |
| **Asset Manager** | Registers new assets, allocates equipment, and acts as the approver for asset transfers, maintenance requests, returns, and audit resolutions. |
| **Department Head** | Oversees assets assigned to their specific department, approves internal transfers, and books shared resources on behalf of their team. |
| **Employee** | Views personally allocated assets, books shared resources, reports issues via maintenance requests, and initiates asset transfers or returns. |

## 🔄 Typical Workflow

1. **System Setup:** The Admin configures departments and asset categories, then assigns managerial roles to specific employees.
2. **Asset Onboarding:** Asset Managers register new inventory (e.g., laptops, projectors), which enter the system as *Available*.
3. **Allocation & Booking:** Assets are directly allocated to staff, while shared resources (like meeting rooms) are reserved by users via the calendar booking system.
4. **Maintenance & Support:** If an item breaks, the holder raises a maintenance request. Once approved, the asset status shifts to *Under Maintenance*.
5. **Auditing & Reporting:** The organization runs periodic audit cycles to verify inventory health, locking the cycles once finished and updating the system with any missing or damaged items.
